If you're thinking about cheap tropical islands to visit on your own, no travel agency, here are the exotic places that I keep recommending to people. These are also, in my opinion, the best tropical islands to visit if you want the real thing, not a resort bubble.</p>

<p>The world has no shortage of beautiful tropical islands, but some are genuinely different from everything else. These are the ones that stuck with me.</p>

<h2 id="hawaii-united-states">Hawaii, United States 🇺🇸</h2>

<p><strong>Hawaii</strong> quickly became the most beautiful tropical island destination I've ever visited: perfect beaches, volcanoes and green mountains, charming little towns, ideal weather year-round, and all the comfort of a developed country.</p>

<h2 id="seychelles">Seychelles 🇸🇨</h2>

<p><strong>The Seychelles</strong> are famous, and rightly so. The best beaches I've ever seen are there, with those iconic boulders scattered all over the place. The islands also have mountains, not just beaches, so you get the full package for an exotic destination: stunning shorelines, hills to explore, great food, and friendly people.</p>

<h2 id="mauritius">Mauritius 🇲🇺</h2>

<p>Not far from <strong>Seychelles</strong> is another stunning island for an exotic getaway: <strong>Mauritius</strong>. From <strong>Le Morne</strong> you get a spectacular view of the coral reef with its countless shades of blue, and the island has waterfalls, beaches, temples, and beautiful sunsets. <strong>Mauritius</strong> is something else.</p>

<h2 id="saint-lucia">Saint Lucia 🇱🇨</h2>

<p><em>Ok</em>, I think there's a pattern here: I love tropical islands that also have mountains, and when those mountains happen to be two beautiful Pitons, it can only be a stunning destination.</p>

<p><strong>Saint Lucia</strong> is an incredibly lush island, with thick green vegetation that feels like a small paradise you walk through before ending your day on the shore of the <strong>Caribbean Sea</strong>. The beaches may not be quite on par with <strong>Seychelles</strong>, but <strong>Saint Lucia</strong> is a wonderful destination regardless.</p>

<h2 id="palawan-philippines">Palawan, Philippines 🇵🇭</h2>

<p>Exotic Asian islands have a distinct appeal, wilder and less polished than what you find in the Caribbean. One of the best places in Asia for cheap exotic island hopping is the <strong>Philippines</strong>. <strong>Palawan</strong> has <strong>El Nido</strong>, the most beautiful place I've seen in that part of the world when it comes to islands, beaches, and mountains. 45 islands and islets to explore by boat, bays to paddle through by canoe, caves, and secret beaches — <strong>El Nido</strong> is not to be missed.</p>

<h2 id="galapagos-ecuador">Galápagos, Ecuador 🇪🇨</h2>

<p>Looking for exotic destinations with a twist, maybe in <strong><a href="https://www.1001calatorii.ro/category/america-de-sud/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">South America</a></strong>? Some of the best beaches I've ever seen were in the <strong>Galápagos</strong>: while elsewhere you find commercialized shorelines, in the <strong>Galápagos</strong> 97% of the land is national park, so it's just you, the iguanas, and the sea lions.</p>

<p>Wide beaches with fine white sand, tree cacti, and the raw beauty of the edge of the world — <strong>Galápagos</strong> is a place you need to visit in this lifetime.</p>

<h2 id="hispaniola-dominican-republic">Hispaniola, Dominican Republic 🇩🇴</h2>

<p>If you want the quintessential Caribbean beach experience — palm trees leaning just so, white sand, coral washed ashore — the <strong>Dominican Republic</strong> delivers. <strong>Saona</strong> and <strong>Punta Cana</strong> have beautiful beaches, and the <strong>Samaná</strong> peninsula offers completely different scenery, reminiscent of the <strong>Philippines</strong>, like <strong>Playa Frontón</strong>.</p>

<h2 id="guadeloupe">Guadeloupe 🇫🇷</h2>

<p><strong>Guadeloupe</strong> was the surprise of a two-month trip through the <strong>Caribbean</strong>: lesser known outside of <strong>France</strong>, but gorgeous. The <strong>Les Saintes</strong> archipelago is picture perfect.</p>

<h2 id="curacao">Curaçao 🇨🇼</h2>

<p><strong>Curaçao</strong> has the most turquoise water you'll ever see, and <strong>Willemstad</strong> looks like a Caribbean <strong><a href="https://www.1001calatorii.ro/amsterdam-oras-viu/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Amsterdam</a></strong> — and honestly much prettier than the capital of <strong>Aruba</strong>.</p>

<h2 id="zanzibar-tanzania">Zanzibar, Tanzania 🇹🇿</h2>

<p>Despite being a very flat island, <strong>Zanzibar</strong> transforms at low tide, when dozens of meters of beach are exposed and reveal colors and sea creatures until the next tide rolls in. It's a popular exotic destination — you'll find restaurants perched on small rocky outcrops, the striking <strong>Maasai</strong>, and the fascinating <strong>Stone Town</strong>.</p>

<h2 id="bali-indonesia">Bali, Indonesia 🇮🇩</h2>

<p><strong>Bali</strong> is the kind of island that has everything: beaches, temples, mountains, rice paddies, great food, beautiful guesthouses, friendly people, waterfalls — and I could keep going. In the end, it wasn't the beaches that got me, it was the interior of the island, with all its stunning corners, which I still hadn't gotten tired of after a full month.</p>

<p><strong>Aruba</strong> had the most incredibly turquoise and transparent water I've ever seen in the tropics. The sand is very white, the water is clean and full of tropical fish, and the sky is an almost unreal shade of blue all day long. <strong>Aruba</strong> is part of the <strong>Kingdom of the Netherlands</strong>, so there's a touch of Dutch charm mixed in with all those perfect beaches.</p>

<h2 id="maldives">Maldives 🇲🇻</h2>

<p>The <strong>Maldives</strong> wouldn't be my first pick for an exotic vacation, but if you don't mind staying on one of the hundreds of tiny islands there, you'll get tropical beaches, sandbars that emerge at low tide, and plenty of luxury accommodations.</p>

<p>The archipelago is extremely fragmented and traveling between islands is a challenge (visiting them all isn't even a realistic idea — there are 1,192 coral islands grouped into 26 natural atolls!), so you'll most likely end up staying on just the island where you're based.</p>
